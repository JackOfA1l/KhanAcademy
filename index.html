Quality-Cost-Time Tradeoffs

High Quality vs. Faster Delivery

High quality is hard to define, and we can keep refining content endlessly. Perfect content doesn't exist - there's always room for improvement. The focus should be on delivering content faster, as long as it meets key quality metrics like accuracy, clarity, curriculum alignment, and engagement.

Internal Team vs. Freelancers

Internal teams and freelancers both have their strengths and weaknesses. Internal teams provide better control and steady quality. Freelancers help with speed and flexibility. The optimal approach is to have a strong internal team while working with trusted freelancers when needed.

Video-based vs. Other Formats

Video-based teaching is more engaging as it uses both visuals and sound. Studies show it often leads to better understanding than just reading articles. Additionally, once videos are created, it's possible to train a model to transcribe and convert them into articles, making content creation faster and more versatile.

Cost Saving Strategies

Prioritize High-Impact Grades or Subjects First

Focus on Grades 9‚Äì10 or core subjects like Maths and Science to get the most value from limited resources.

Use Freely Available Open Educational Resources (OER)

Adapt content from trusted open sources like CK-12 or NCERT instead of building everything from scratch.

Use AI Tools to Speed Up Drafting

Automate tasks like practice set creation, quiz generation, and proofreading using AI tools to save time, allowing the team to focus on review and content improvement.
